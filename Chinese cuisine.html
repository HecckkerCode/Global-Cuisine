Chinese Cuisine

Chinese cuisine is one of the worlds' most diverse and richly flavored culinary traditions, known for its variety of ingredients, cooking techniques, and regional specialties. Chinese meals include four main food groups — fruits, vegetables, grains and meat. These dishes does not contain large amounts of dairy. Instead, they typically include calcium- and protein-rich alternatives like tofu and soymilk.

Traditional and Modern Chinese Cuisine

Chinese regional cuisine is a blend of traditional dishes that have been enjoyed for centuries, reflecting the country's agricultural heritage, social customs, and regional diversity. While often known for its heteay, straightforward meals, Chinese cuisine also incorporates influences from its colonial past and global connections. Each region in China has its own cooking and dining habits due to geographical and climate differences. Finding foods based on your bodys' needs in relation to geographical location is ptea of the yin and yang philosophy.

Cooking Techniques with
recipes

Chinese cuisine is known for its diverse and intricate cooking techniques, each designed to bring out the best flavors, textures, and aromas in the ingredients. Here's an overview of some of the most prominent cooking techniques used in Chinese cooking. Different ingredients are cooked using different methods, while the same ingredient can be used in different dishes to provide different flavors and appearances. There are hundreds of cooking methods in China. However, the most common methods are stir-frying, deep-frying, shallow-frying, braising, boiling, steaming and roasting.

  • Stir-Frying(Chǎo)
  • Steaming(Zhēng)
  • Braising and Stewing(Hóngshāo)
  • Deep-frying(Zhà)
